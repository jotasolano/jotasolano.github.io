Inaugural

A visual exploration of presidential inauguration addresses from 1789 to 2009

The presidential addresses set the tone for the new administration of the republic. This is a visual exploration of the words, concepts and ideas that are discussed, disected by their frequency across fifty-six presidencies. The visualizations seek to discover trends in tone and ideas across time, both positive and negative, and to reveal hidden messages embedded in the narratives. How was this created?

Concepts and frequency

This chart explores six different concepts that range from positive to negative connotations. The concepts are a cluster of cognitive synonyms, formally known as synsets. These synonyms are displayed under each concept.

Each line shows the minimum and maximum mentions of the concept. Hover over any line for more information.

Positive and negative concepts across time

The concepts are proxies of the tone and content of every speech. By visualizing them across time it is possible to see the differences between every presidency and to reflec on the historical context of each inauguration. Click on any graphic for more detail.

About the data and methods

The data was obtained from the NLTK Python Package corpus database. Additionally, using the same library and NLTK's WordNet Synset Interface it was possible to come up with a a group of cognitive synonyms for every concept. Then frequencies were obtained and normalized according to the lenght of every speech.


The data was further cleaned and prepared in R and then visualized using d3.js


This project was created by Antonio Solano for the Studio II course, at the Masters in Information Design and Visualization at Northeastern University.
